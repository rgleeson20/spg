About Sarah Gleeson

Sarah Gleeson is a visual artist from Cork, Ireland who has made art her life's passion. She attended art college in her early teens and, at the same time, began teaching art part time in local schools to support herself. She then left for London to pursue a career in design. She returned to Ireland to start a family which took a priority in her life but her passion for art never abated and she continued drawing and painting in here spare time. The next chapter in her life began in 2003 when she pursued an art course in art craft and design in Cork's esteemed college, Coláiste Stiofáin Naofa. During this time, her work she was chosen to join a group exhibition at the Tigh Filí cultural centre.

After gaining many new skills and a new outlook on the artistic scene, she decided to attend Crawford College of Art and Design, one of Ireland's most prestigious Art colleges. She graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in 2010. Since then, she has been asked to exhibit in many group exhibitions and was chosen to display her paintings in the windows of upmarket department store, Brown Thomas.

Through her work, Sarah combines a variety of materials and techniques. This diversity reflects the different aspects of her personality and her many varied interests. One of her abiding interests is to capture the beautiful and storied buildings of Cork, including the English Market and Blarney Castle.
